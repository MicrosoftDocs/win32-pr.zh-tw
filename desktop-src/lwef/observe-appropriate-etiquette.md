<span data-ttu-id="9935f-108">我們預期互動中的 reciprocity，而不會發生這種情況時，我們通常會將行為視為 incompetent 或冒犯性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-108">We expect reciprocity in our interaction and when it doesn't happen we tend to view the behavior as incompetent or offensive.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-109">在新的或正式的情況下，通常會需要禮貌程度。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-109">In new or formal situations, politeness is usually expected.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-110">只有在建立關聯性之後，才有熟悉的互動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-110">Only after a relationship is established does familiarity allow for less formal interaction.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-111">因此，當您不確定時，請按禮貌。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-111">So when in doubt, be polite.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9935f-112">例如，請考慮使用適當的通訊協定來開始和結束社交互動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-112">For example, consider the appropriate protocol for starting and ending a social interaction.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-113">當您參與新的社交內容時，我們通常會彼此歡迎，並指出我們要在我們偏離之前保留的意圖。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-113">When engaging in a new social context, we generally greet each other and indicate our intent to leave before we depart.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-114">您可以直接將此模型套用至字元互動;避免出現或消失的字元，而不會有適當的指示。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-114">You can apply this model directly to character interaction; avoid the character appearing or disappearing without an appropriate indication.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9935f-115">禮貌程度的一部分是我們的回應方式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-115">A part of politeness is how we respond.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-116">我們預期會有適當的回應。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-116">We expect responses that are appropriate.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-117">例如，如果您問：「這是哪一天？」</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-117">For example, if you ask, "What day is it?"</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-118">您不希望回應「10蘋果」。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-118">you don't expect the response to be "10 apples."</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-119">不過，精確度和相關性不足;您可能也需要判斷內容。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-119">However, accuracy and relevance are not enough; you may also need to determine the context.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-120">精確的回應可能是「星期一」或「8月<sup>25 日</sup>」。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-120">An accurate response could be "Monday" or "August 25<sup>th</sup>."</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-121">同樣地，回應的用語可以是正確的，但仍會有冒犯性，取決於聲調、臉部運算式或其他非口頭行為。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-121">Similarly, the wording of a response can be accurate but still be offensive, depending on intonation, facial expression, or other non-verbal behaviors.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9935f-122">此外，我們會在討論時互相解決：我們面對的是，只是要將注意力導向到東西。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-122">In addition, we address each other while talking: we face the person, turning only to direct attention to something.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-123">重新開啟一回，而不需要某些用途，意指 disinterest，通常視為 impolite。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-123">Turning one's back, without some purpose, implies disinterest and is generally considered to be impolite.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9935f-124">禮貌程度也會影響我們回應或察覺他人的方式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-124">Politeness also affects the way we respond to or perceive others.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-125">例如，「社交研究」指出當其他人詢問他人的要求時，我們比較有可能會對其他人提出評價。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-125">For example, social research indicates that we are more likely to be honest about our evaluation of others when asked by another person than when the people ask about themselves.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-126">同樣地，我們會比正面的自我評估更容易接受其他人的精確評估。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-126">Similarly, we accept positive evaluations of others more readily than positive self-evaluations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-127">Immodest 行為通常視為 impolite。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-127">Immodest behavior is generally considered impolite.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-128">因此，除非刻意嘗試升級 impolite 的特質樣式，否則請避免字元補充其本身的效能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-128">Therefore, unless intentionally trying to promote an impolite personality style, avoid having the character compliment its own performance.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9935f-129">也請考慮文化對社交禮儀的影響。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-129">Also consider the cultural impact on social etiquette.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-130">這可能適用于字元的服裝、手勢或單字選擇。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-130">This may apply to the character's attire, gestures, or word choice.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9935f-131">雖然有禮貌的行為可能因文化特性而異，但禮貌程度的概念是跨文化。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9935f-131">While polite behaviors may vary by culture, the concept of politeness is cross-cultural.</span></span>
